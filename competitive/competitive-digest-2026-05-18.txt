## FLORIDA MARKET PULSE
Adult-use remains the dominant storyline. Amendment 3 implementation timelines continue driving operator positioning — everyone is building toward recreational infrastructure while still operating medical. Price compression persists statewide; average flower prices down roughly 8–12% YoY as cultivators push inventory ahead of the transition. Regulators at OMMU are processing dispensary location applications at an accelerated clip. Retail footprint expansion is the current arms race.

**Curaleaf** — Continued to rationalize underperforming locations nationally; Florida remains a protected priority market for them. No major new openings flagged last week, but their wholesale/white-label positioning suggests they're hedging for post-reclassification supply dynamics.

**Surterra/Parallel** — Financial instability chatter hasn't gone away. Operational focus appears narrowed. Could represent a store acquisition opportunity or a cautionary tale — watch their license activity.

**VidaCann** — Relatively quiet. Maintaining a niche, premium-adjacent positioning. Not a volume threat but competes for the quality-conscious patient segment Planet 13 targets.

**Fluent** — Small footprint, holding steady. Not aggressively expanding. Less of a near-term competitive concern.
